The Toyota Prius is one of the most common hybrids on Bangladesh's roads, and after years of service the high-voltage battery is the typical age-related fault owners run into. The good news is that a failing Prius pack rarely fails all at once — with the right diagnosis, many cars can be put right without unnecessary expense.

Symptoms of a failing Prius battery

If your Prius is showing any of the following, the high-voltage battery is a likely culprit and worth testing:

  • The red triangle warning (the "red triangle of death") on the dash
  • The hybrid-system warning light appearing alongside it
  • A stored P0A80 code — "Replace Hybrid Battery Pack"
  • A big drop in fuel economy — the engine works harder to compensate
  • Reduced power and weaker acceleration, especially in traffic
  • The petrol engine running almost constantly instead of switching to electric drive

None of these are a death sentence on their own — they are signals to get the pack tested properly before deciding what to do.

Recondition or replace?

We give you the honest answer, and the only way to reach it is a proper health test. A Prius pack is made up of many cell modules. If most modules are healthy and only a few are weak or out of balance, cell- or module-level reconditioning can restore performance for a fraction of the cost of a new pack. If the pack is broadly degraded, replacement is the right call and we'll say so.

Diagnosis decides — not guesswork. It's worth knowing that Dhaka's heat shortens hybrid-pack life compared with cooler climates, so even a reconditioned pack benefits from regular checks afterwards.

Our process

  • Hybrid scan — we read the hybrid control system and confirm the stored fault codes.
  • Block-voltage / health test — we measure each module to find weak cells and check pack balance.
  • Recommendation — recondition, replace specific modules, or replace the pack, based on the data.
  • Clear quote — you approve the cost before any work starts.
  • Service & post-check — we carry out the work and re-test the pack to confirm the fix.

Safety first: a Prius battery runs at dangerous high voltage. It must only ever be handled by trained professionals with the correct equipment — please don't attempt DIY work on the pack.
