In Dhaka's heat and traffic, a working air-conditioning system isn't a luxury — it's essential. On premium European cars the AC is more than a compressor and some gas: it's a fully electronic climate-control system with sensors, actuators and control modules that decide exactly how the cabin is cooled. When it stops cooling, the right answer is rarely a quick refill — it's proper diagnosis of why it failed.

Symptoms of AC problems

Get your system looked at when you notice any of these:

  • Weak or no cooling — the AC runs but the cabin never gets cold
  • Warm air blowing from the vents even on the coldest setting
  • A damp or musty smell when the AC is switched on
  • Noise or rattling from the compressor when the AC engages
  • Climate control not responding, displaying faults or stuck on one zone
  • Windows fogging up that the system can't clear

Why proper diagnosis beats a quick gas refill

If your AC has stopped cooling, the refrigerant has usually gone somewhere — and that means a leak. Simply topping up the gas without finding the leak cools the car for a few days or weeks, then leaves you exactly where you started, often with an over- or under-charged system that strains the compressor. We leak-test and pressure-test the system, find the actual cause, and fix it — so the repair lasts. Diagnosis first is also the honest approach: you only pay to fix what's genuinely wrong.

Common faults we fix

  • Refrigerant leaks — at hoses, seals, condensers and connections, located properly rather than guessed
  • Compressor faults — failing clutches, bearings and internal wear
  • Condenser damage — common from road debris and corrosion in our climate
  • Expansion valve and dryer — restrictions that kill cooling performance
  • Blower motors and actuators — airflow and flap-control problems
  • Climate-control electronics — CLIMATRONIC, COMAND and similar systems on BMW, Mercedes-Benz and Audi, including sensors and control modules

Our process

Every AC job follows the same disciplined sequence:

  • Inspect — visual and electronic check of the full system
  • Leak & pressure test — to confirm whether the system is holding charge
  • Diagnose — identify the real root cause, mechanical or electronic, using full car diagnostics
  • Quote — a clear price before any work starts
  • Repair — fix the cause with the correct parts and method
  • Recharge & verify — evacuate, recharge to specification and confirm proper cooling
